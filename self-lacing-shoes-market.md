<p><p>The self-lacing shoes market can be divided into two types: rechargeable and non-rechargeable self-lacing shoes. Rechargeable self-lacing shoes are equipped with a built-in battery that can be charged, allowing users to lace and tighten the shoes electronically. On the other hand, non-rechargeable self-lacing shoes do not have a battery and instead rely on manual mechanisms to lace and tighten. Both types offer the convenience of automated lacing, but rechargeable shoes require occasional charging for continued functionality, while non-rechargeable shoes do not require any external power source.</p></p>

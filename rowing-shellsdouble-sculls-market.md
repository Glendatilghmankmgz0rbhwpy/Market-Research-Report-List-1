<p><p>Rowing shells, specifically double sculls, are popular in two different market types: coxless pair and coxed pair. In coxless pair, two rowers steer the shell themselves without a coxswain, providing a more challenging and self-reliant experience. On the other hand, coxed pair involves a coxswain steering the shell and providing direction to the rowers. This market type is ideal for those who prefer guidance and teamwork in their rowing experience. Both types offer unique benefits and cater to different preferences within the rowing community.</p></p>

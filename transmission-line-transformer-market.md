<p><p>Transmission line transformers are designed to match the impedance of two connected transmission lines. The impedance ratio of 1:1 means the input and output impedances are equal, providing a perfect impedance match. A ratio of 4:1 or 8:1 means the output impedance is four or eight times higher than the input impedance, respectively. Other types of transmission line transformers may have different impedance ratios to accommodate specific applications or requirements. These transformers play a crucial role in efficiently transferring signals between different impedance systems.</p></p>

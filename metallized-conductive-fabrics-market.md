<p><p>Metallized conductive fabrics are divided into different types based on their construction. Woven conductive fabrics are created by interlacing warp and weft threads, providing strength and durability. Non-woven conductive fabrics are made by bonding or felting fibers together without weaving, offering flexibility and breathability. Other types of metallized conductive fabrics may include knit fabrics or specialty variations with unique features. Each type has its own benefits and is used in various applications within the electronics and aerospace industries.</p></p>

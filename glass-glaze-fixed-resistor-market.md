<p><p>Glass glaze fixed resistors are commonly categorized into three types based on their lead configuration: Radial Lead Type, Axial Lead Type, and Others. Radial Lead Type resistors have leads that are located on opposite ends of the resistor body, making them suitable for easy mounting on circuit boards. Axial Lead Type resistors, on the other hand, have leads on the same side of the body, making them ideal for through-hole applications. The "Others" category may include unique lead configurations or specialized resistors that do not fit into the standard radial or axial types.</p></p>

<p><p>Endothermic chemical foaming agents are divided into two types based on their physical form: powder and granule. Powder forms are finely ground particles that are easily dispersible in polymers, while granule forms are larger particles that provide a slower release of gas during the foaming process. Both types have their own advantages and are used in various applications within industries such as packaging, automotive, and construction. The choice between powder and granule forms depends on specific requirements of the foaming process and desired final product characteristics.</p></p>

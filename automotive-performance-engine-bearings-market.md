Ball Bearings: These bearings have small metal balls within a ring, allowing for smooth rotation and reduced friction.</p><p> </p><p>2. Roller Bearings: These bearings use cylindrical rollers to support rotating components, providing greater load capacity and durability compared to ball bearings.</p><p>3. Plain Bearings: These bearings have a simple design with a sliding surface, ideal for lower speed and high load applications.</p><p>4. Others: This category includes specialized bearings such as spherical bearings or needle bearings designed for specific performance needs in automotive engines.</p></p>

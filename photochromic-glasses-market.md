<p><p>Photochromic glasses are a type of eyewear that automatically darkens in response to sunlight and UV light exposure. There are two main types of photochromic glasses on the market: substrate discoloration glasses and film-changing glasses. Substrate discoloration glasses use a chemical reaction within the lens material to darken, while film-changing glasses have a special film that changes opacity in response to light. Both types provide the convenience of transitioning from clear indoor lenses to sunglasses outdoors, offering UV protection and comfort.</p></p>

<p><p>Bio-Polylactic Acid (PLA) is a biodegradable polymer derived from renewable resources like corn starch or sugarcane. There are three main types of PLA based on the chirality of the lactic acid molecule: Poly(L-lactic)acid, Poly(D-lactic)acid, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid. Poly(L-lactic)acid is derived from L-lactic acid molecules, Poly(D-lactic)acid from D-lactic acid molecules, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid from a mix of both L and D-lactic acid molecules. Each type of PLA offers different properties and applications in industries like packaging, textiles, and biomedical devices.</p></p>

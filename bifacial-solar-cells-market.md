<p><p>Bifacial solar cells are divided into two main market types based on the type of material used for the cells: N-type and P-type. N-type bifacial solar cells use a negatively charged silicon layer as the base material, while P-type cells utilize a positively charged silicon layer. Both types have their own advantages and applications in the solar energy industry. N-type cells are known for their higher efficiency and radiation resistance, while P-type cells are more commonly used due to their lower production cost and availability.</p></p>

<p><p>RF power amplifiers (PAs) are designed to increase the power of RF signals for transmission purposes, making them essential components in wireless communication systems. On the other hand, RF low noise amplifiers (LNAs) are used to amplify weak signals with minimal noise interference, making them crucial in receiving and processing signals accurately. Both types of amplifier chips play vital roles in ensuring efficient and reliable performance of RF/microwave systems in various applications such as telecommunications, radar, and satellite communication.</p></p>

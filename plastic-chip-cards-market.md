<p><p>Plastic chip cards come in two main market types: contact cards and contactless cards. Contact cards require physical contact between the card and a card reader for data exchange, while contactless cards use radio frequency technology for data transmission without physical contact. Contact cards are commonly used in applications where security is crucial, such as banking and identification. Contactless cards are becoming increasingly popular for their convenience and speed of transactions, particularly in public transportation and retail settings.</p></p>

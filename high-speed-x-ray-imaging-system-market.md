<p><p>High speed X-ray imaging systems can be classified into two main categories based on their frame rates: those that operate at less than 30 frames per second (fps) and those that operate at more than 30 fps. The market for systems operating at less than 30 fps per second typically caters to applications that require slower motion capture, such as industrial inspection and non-destructive testing. On the other hand, systems operating at more than 30 fps per second are commonly used in dynamic imaging applications that involve fast-moving objects or processes, such as material deformation analysis and sports biomechanics.</p></p>
